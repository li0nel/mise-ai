Instructions
1
Make the curry paste: Drain the soaked dried chillies. Toast coriander seeds and cumin seeds in a dry pan over medium heat for ⏱ 2 min until fragrant. Let cool, then grind to a powder in a spice grinder or mortar.
2
Combine all paste ingredients in a mortar or food processor: drained chillies, ground spices, garlic, lemongrass, shallots, galangal, roasted shrimp paste, and salt. Pound or blend until a smooth, fragrant paste forms. ⏱ 10–15 min
💡 A mortar and pestle gives a coarser, more texturally complex paste than a blender. If using a blender, add a splash of coconut milk to help it move.
3
In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat 3 tbsp of the thick cream from the top of a coconut milk can over medium-high heat. Add the curry paste and fry, stirring constantly, for ⏱ 4–5 min until darkened, fragrant, and oil has separated from the paste.
⚠️ Don't skip frying the paste — raw paste tastes muddy. You want it to deepen in colour and the oil to visibly separate from the solids.
4
Add the beef chuck cubes to the pot. Stir to coat every piece in the paste. Sear for ⏱ 3 min until the outside of the beef is sealed and lightly coloured.
5
Pour in the remaining coconut milk. Add cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and bay leaves. Stir to combine. Bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce heat to low.
6
Cover and simmer on the lowest possible heat for ⏱ 1 hour. Stir every 15–20 minutes. The beef should begin to tenderise but is not yet ready.
🕐 The curry deepens in flavour and colour during this time. Resist the urge to crank up the heat — low and slow is everything for this dish.
7
Add the baby potatoes and quartered onions. Stir in half the peanuts, fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind paste. Continue to simmer uncovered for another ⏱ 45–60 min until beef is fork-tender and sauce has thickened.
8
Taste and adjust the balance: more fish sauce for salt, palm sugar for sweetness, tamarind for sour. The flavour should be simultaneously rich, sweet, slightly sour, and deeply savoury — with the aromatic spices in the background.
9
Remove the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and bay leaves. Scatter the remaining roasted peanuts over the top. The curry should be thick enough to coat a spoon.
10
Serve over jasmine rice or roti canai. Garnish with additional peanuts, a swirl of coconut cream, and fresh coriander. Provide extra fish sauce and chilli on the side.
✨ Massaman tastes dramatically better the next day. Make it ahead — the flavours bloom and deepen overnight in the fridge.
